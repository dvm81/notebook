The Federal Reserve held interest rates steady at its November meeting, in line with market expectations. Chair Powell emphasized the Committee's data-dependent approach while noting inflation remains above the 2% target. Treasury yields rose following the announcement.

NVIDIA reported record quarterly revenue of $18.1 billion, up 206% year-over-year, driven by surging demand for AI chips. The company's data center segment posted $14.5 billion in sales. Management guided fourth-quarter revenue to approximately $20 billion.

Tesla shares declined 3% after the company reported Q3 deliveries below analyst estimates. The automaker cited production challenges and supply chain disruptions. CEO Elon Musk stated on social media that the company remains on track for its annual delivery target.

Apple unveiled its new iPhone 15 lineup with improved camera systems and upgraded processors. Pre-orders begin Friday, with devices shipping next week. Analysts expect strong initial demand, particularly for the Pro models. The base model starts at $799.

Microsoft announced a $60 billion share buyback program and raised its quarterly dividend by 10%. The tech giant reported cloud revenue growth of 27% in the most recent quarter. CEO Satya Nadella highlighted expanding AI capabilities across the product portfolio.
