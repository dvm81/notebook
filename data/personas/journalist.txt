Walmart announced plans to equip 65% of its U.S. stores with AI-powered systems by 2026, investing $9 billion in the initiative. The technology includes autonomous robots, AI inventory cameras, and automated fulfillment centers. CEO Doug McMillon emphasized that the program focuses on workforce redeployment rather than headcount reduction. Pilot programs demonstrated 20% cost reductions.

Commercial office vacancy rates reached 19.6% across major U.S. metropolitan areas, marking 20-year highs according to CBRE. San Francisco leads at 31.8%, followed by Houston and Chicago. The trend reflects lasting impacts of remote work policies. Some landlords are converting office buildings to residential use, though conversions face regulatory and financial challenges.

The Bureau of Ocean Energy Management will conduct the largest U.S. offshore wind lease auction in March 2025, offering eight areas totaling 488,000 acres. The sites could generate 5.6 gigawatts, supporting the administration's 30 GW by 2030 target. Companies must commit to community benefit agreements. The announcement comes amid industry challenges including project cancellations by major developers.

Google Quantum AI achieved a milestone in quantum error correction, demonstrating that error rates decrease as qubit grids expand. The Willow chip contains 105 qubits and can perform certain calculations exponentially faster than classical computers. However, experts note that practical applications remain years away as current systems solve only specialized problems.

The U.S. and Japan will establish a joint defense command structure by 2025, the most significant alliance upgrade since 1960. The permanent joint headquarters will improve coordination between U.S. Forces Japan and the Japan Self-Defense Forces. The agreement responds to regional security concerns including Taiwan Strait tensions and North Korean missile programs.
